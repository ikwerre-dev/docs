## Redemption Periods, Grace Periods & Registry Actions

Following a domain's expiration, most TLD registries enter the domain into a grace period (typically 0–45 days) during which it may be renewed at standard cost. After the grace period, the domain enters a Redemption Grace Period (RGP), typically lasting 30 days, during which the domain can only be recovered at significantly higher redemption fees charged directly by the registry. Pxxl will pass these fees on to you without markup where possible, but they are non-negotiable and non-refundable.

After the redemption period, the domain enters a Pending Delete phase (typically 5 days) and will be permanently deleted and released to the public for re-registration. Pxxl cannot recover a domain once it reaches Pending Delete status. We assume no liability for any loss resulting from failure to renew before these deadlines.

Certain TLD registries reserve the right to suspend, cancel, or reassign domain names for policy violations, trademark complaints, court orders, or other registry-specific reasons without prior notice to Pxxl or to you. Pxxl shall not be held responsible for any such registry-imposed actions. You acknowledge and accept this risk as part of the domain registration process.
