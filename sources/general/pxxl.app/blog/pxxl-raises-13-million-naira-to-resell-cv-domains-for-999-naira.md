Pxxl has raised ₦13 million, about $10,000, to make .cv domains radically more accessible for builders, students, creators, agencies, and small businesses. The goal is simple: let anyone buy a clean .cv domain for just ₦999 and have it ready for real use in seconds.

This is not just a price drop. It is an infrastructure move. Domains have always been one of those tiny steps that somehow turn into a long afternoon: find a registrar, pay in dollars, wait for fraud checks, copy nameservers, wait for DNS, configure SSL, connect the project, then hope nothing silently breaks. Pxxl is removing that mess from the workflow.

## Why .cv matters

The .cv extension has a natural meaning for careers, portfolios, founders, operators, engineers, designers, writers, and anyone who needs a sharp personal or professional presence online. A designer can use ada.cv. A backend engineer can use john.cv. A startup recruiter can use talent.cv. A student can use grace.cv for a portfolio before sending applications. It is short, memorable, and flexible enough to work for both people and products.

For African builders, the problem has not been imagination. The problem has been access. Domain pricing is often shown in dollars, checkout flows can reject local cards, and the setup experience assumes the customer already understands DNS. Pxxl is attacking all three problems at once: local pricing, local payment, and instant setup.

## What the ₦13 million raise is funding

The raise gives Pxxl enough operating room to resell .cv domains at ₦999 while absorbing the infrastructure work needed to make the purchase feel instant. That includes registry integration, DNS automation, payment reconciliation, domain task workers, SSL provisioning, proxy routing, support tooling, and the boring reliability work that makes a one-click product feel calm.

Under the hood, Pxxl does a lot after checkout. It validates the invoice, creates or links the registrar contact, registers the domain, creates the DNS zone, adds the right records, connects the domain to the selected project, syncs the global proxy, and prepares HTTPS. The customer sees one clean flow. The platform handles the stack.

## From checkout to live in seconds

The promise is not only that .cv domains are cheaper. The promise is that they are useful immediately. When a domain is bought through Pxxl, the platform can manage the DNS directly, which means there is no need to copy nameservers from one dashboard to another just to make a project work.

After purchase, a user can connect the domain to a project, add subdomains, point a route to a deployment, and let Pxxl handle the proxy and certificate work. A personal site, landing page, API, portfolio, or agency client preview can move from idea to public URL without leaving the dashboard.

A domain changes how seriously people treat a project. A GitHub Pages link feels temporary. A generated subdomain feels like a preview. A proper domain makes the project feel intentional. At ₦999, that step becomes small enough for experiments, hackathon projects, class assignments, personal brands, client demos, and weekend ideas.

That matters because many products start as tiny experiments. A ₦999 domain lets a builder test an idea without turning the purchase into a budget decision. It also lets agencies create sharper demos for clients without passing around raw deployment URLs. The emotional difference is real: people click a real domain with more trust.

## The Pxxl way: domain, DNS, SSL, and deployment together

Pxxl is not treating domains as a separate checkout page bolted onto hosting. Domains are part of the deployment system. That means the domain view can show DNS records, route status, project connections, subdomains, invoices, and setup progress in one place. It also means resyncing a route can speak to the proxy and refresh the certificate path without asking the user to understand the moving parts.

For .cv domains bought on Pxxl, the default experience is Pxxl-managed DNS. Users can still manage records when they need to, but the happy path is automatic. Buy the domain, connect the project, ship. That is the product principle.
