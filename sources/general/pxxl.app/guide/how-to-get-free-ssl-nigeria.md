# How to Get a Free SSL Certificate for Your Website in Nigeria

SSL certificates make your website secure (HTTPS) and trustworthy to Nigerian users and Google's ranking algorithm. This guide explains how to get a free SSL certificate for any website or web application.

## Why Every Nigerian Website Needs HTTPS

Google marks HTTP websites as 'Not Secure' in Chrome, which reduces trust and can deter Nigerian visitors from using your application. HTTPS also protects user data — login credentials, form submissions, payment details — from interception. Search engines including Google rank HTTPS sites higher than HTTP equivalents, making SSL important for SEO in the Nigerian market too.

## How Pxxl Provides Free SSL Automatically

Every Pxxl deployment — including the free \*.pxxl.app subdomain — comes with automatic HTTPS enabled. When you connect a custom domain, Pxxl uses Let's Encrypt to issue a free Domain Validated SSL certificate within minutes of DNS verification. There is no manual configuration, no purchasing a certificate from a Certificate Authority, and no annual renewal to worry about. Pxxl renews your certificate automatically before it expires.

## Troubleshooting SSL Issues in Nigeria

If your custom domain shows an SSL error after adding it to Pxxl, first verify that your DNS records are correctly set by checking with a tool like dnschecker.org. SSL certificate issuance requires that Pxxl's servers can reach your domain, so ensure the A record points to Pxxl's IP. If you recently transferred a domain from another registrar in Nigeria, wait 24-48 hours for full DNS propagation before expecting the certificate to be issued.
