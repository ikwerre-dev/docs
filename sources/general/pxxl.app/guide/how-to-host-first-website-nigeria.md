# How to Host Your First Website as a Nigerian Developer — Step-by-Step Guide

Hosting your first website is one of the most exciting milestones in a developer's journey. This guide walks Nigerian developers through every step — from writing your code to having a live, publicly accessible URL — using Pxxl's free tier.

## Step 1: Prepare Your Website Files

Create a folder for your project and add your HTML, CSS, and JavaScript files. Your main file should be named index.html. Push your project to a GitHub repository by running 'git init', 'git add .', 'git commit -m "first commit"', and 'git push origin main'. If you don't have a GitHub account, create one free at github.com — it takes two minutes.

## Step 2: Deploy on Pxxl

Visit pxxl.app and sign up with your GitHub account. Click 'New Project' and select the repository you just pushed. Pxxl will detect your static site automatically and configure the build. Click 'Deploy' and within 30 seconds your site will be live at a \*.pxxl.app URL. Share this URL with anyone — it works immediately with HTTPS already configured.

## Step 3: Connect a Custom Domain

If you have purchased a domain (domains are available from ₦3,000/year on many Nigerian registrars), go to your project settings in Pxxl, click 'Custom Domain', and enter your domain name. Pxxl will show you DNS records to add at your registrar. After adding the records, Pxxl automatically issues a free SSL certificate. Within 24 hours, your website is live at your own domain with HTTPS.
