# Best Heroku Alternative for Nigerian Developers in 2026

Heroku was the original platform-as-a-service that democratised backend deployment, but after killing its free tier and moving fully to paid plans in USD, it has become inaccessible to most Nigerian developers. Pxxl picks up where Heroku left off.

## What Happened to Heroku's Free Tier

In 2022, Heroku shut down its free dynos and free Postgres databases, forcing thousands of developers — especially students and indie developers in Africa — to find alternatives. Heroku's paid plans start at prices that, converted to Naira, represent significant monthly costs for solo developers and small teams building their first products.

## Pxxl as the Modern Heroku Alternative for Nigeria

Pxxl restores what made Heroku great for early-stage developers: push code, app deploys. It supports the same Procfile-style configuration and handles Node.js, Python, PHP, Ruby, and Go applications. Unlike Heroku, Pxxl's free tier stays free for personal projects, and upgrades are priced fairly for Nigerian developers.

## Migrating a Heroku App to Pxxl

Most Heroku apps migrate to Pxxl without code changes. Connect your GitHub repository, set the same environment variables from Heroku's config vars, and configure your start command. For Heroku Postgres databases, export your data using pg\_dump and import it into a Pxxl-provisioned PostgreSQL instance. Your app will be live on a new URL within minutes.
